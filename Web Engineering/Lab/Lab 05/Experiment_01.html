Experiment 1: Understanding UI/UX Principles

Analysis of Consistency, Feedback, Affordance, Simplicity, and Accessibility

Consistency

Uniform elements & behaviors.

Feedback

Response to user actions.

Affordance

Visual cues for function.

Simplicity

Intuitive & clean layout.

Accessibility

Inclusivity for all users.

Platform Good Practice Bad Practice Principle Applied
Amazon One-click checkout buttons with clear hierarchy. Navigation menus can be overly cluttered/complex. Affordance
Netflix Video previews start automatically (immediate engagement). Autoplay can be intrusive and break user control. Feedback
Airbnb Minimalist search bar with high contrast labels. Filtering options can be hidden too deep in mobile views. Simplicity

Findings Summary

Analyzing platforms revealed that while Amazon excels in affordance through button design, it often compromises Simplicity for the sake of upselling features. In contrast, Netflix uses Consistency to ensure that the mobile and TV experiences feel identical.
